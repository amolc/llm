# LinkedIn Lead Discovery: Step-by-Step Execution Guide

This guide explains how to practically execute the discovery signals mentioned in [lead_generation_strategy.md](file:///Users/amolc/2026/llm/linkedinresearch/lead_generation_strategy.md#L9-14).

## Signal A: Finding the "Complainer"
**Goal**: Find people struggling with current market conditions or manual trading.

1.  **Use the LinkedIn Search Bar**:
    *   Enter keywords like: `"market volatility"`, `"drawdown"`, `"loss"`, `"manual trading"`, `"trading errors"`.
    *   Press Enter.
2.  **Filter by "Posts"**:
    *   Click the **Posts** tab at the top.
    *   Click **All Filters** -> **Sort by: Latest** to find recent discussions.
3.  **Scan for High Engagement**:
    *   Look for posts with **10+ comments**.
    *   Open the comments and look for phrases like:
        *   "I got stopped out again."
        *   "My portfolio is down 20%, I don't know why."
        *   "I wish I had a way to automate this."
4.  **Identify the Lead**:
    *   Click on the profile of the person commenting.
    *   Check their "About" or "Experience" section. If they are a **Fund Manager** or **HNWI**, they are a high-value lead.

## Signal B: Targeting the "Competitor's Audience"
**Goal**: Find people who already use or are interested in similar tools (Portfolio Visualizer, Koyfin, QuantConnect).

1.  **Search for the Competitor**:
    *   Search for `"Portfolio Visualizer"`, `"Koyfin"`, or `"QuantConnect"`.
    *   Select the **Companies** tab and follow them.
2.  **Monitor Their Posts**:
    *   Go to their **Posts** tab.
    *   Look for their most popular recent posts (usually "Feature Updates" or "Market Reports").
3.  **Analyze the Comments**:
    *   Look for people asking **"How do I do X?"** or **"Does this tool support Y?"**.
    *   These people are actively looking for solutions and are likely frustrated by the competitor's complexity or missing features.
4.  **Action**:
    *   Send a connection request with a message mentioning the specific feature they were asking about.

## Signal C: Group Mining (The "Gold Mine")
**Goal**: Find high-seniority professionals in specialized niches.

1.  **Search for Niche Groups**:
    *   Search for: `"Quantitative Finance Professionals"`, `"Algorithmic Trading Wealth"`, `"Systematic Trading"`.
    *   Filter by **Groups** and request to join.
2.  **Access the Member List**:
    *   Once your request is accepted, go to the group page.
    *   Click on the **Members** link (usually under the group name).
3.  **Use the "Search Member" Feature**:
    *   LinkedIn allows you to search *within* group members.
    *   Search for titles like: `CIO`, `Portfolio Manager`, `Director of Research`, `Founder`.
4.  **Action**:
    *   Do **NOT** message them directly from the group (looks like spam).
    *   Instead, find a recent post they made (check their "Activity" tab) and leave a thoughtful comment first. Then send a connection request.
